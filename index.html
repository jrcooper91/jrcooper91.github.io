Current Projects

Jenn Cooper is a Madison & Lila Self Graduate Fellow & PhD Candidate in Physics at the University of Kansas. This is a snapshot of of her current involvement in a variety of projects and organizations.

Icon

Cosmic Environment & Galaxy Evolution

Life & death in the cosmos: how do dense environments such as galaxy clusters affect their ability to survive and create new stars? This study investigates the hydrogen alpha content of galaxies in 3 environments (cluster core, infall, & field) at a redshift ~ 0.5 using the Hubble Space Telescope. Results are in progress and will be published soon.

Icon

Machine Learning in Astronomy

How can machine learning automate and improve our classification of the gas morphology of galaxies? Image classification is an efficient and accurate method to discern between artifacts, noisy data, and usable sources. This study will be trained on 3DHST (z ~ 1) and GLASS (0.3 < z < 0.7), the latter of which has a 10 orbit depth and will allow for gas morphology classification.

Icon

2019-2020 APECS President

Jenn is currently the President of the Association of Polar Early Career Scientists, an organization that focuses on providing opportunities for early career polar scientists around the globe through capacity building, being on the cutting edge of information assessment, and education and outreach. She previously served as Vice President (2018-2019) and Council Co-Chair (2017-2018) and currently sits on the US National Committee Board of USAPECS.

Kansas City University Venture Partnership

This unique program is sponsored by Royal Street Ventures, the Kauffman Foundation, and other private donors to give KC university students experience with private finance, including angel investing, venture capital, growth equity and private equity. Jenn will begin her position in Fall 2020.
